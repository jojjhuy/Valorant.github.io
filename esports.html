Valorant's Esport

    Early Competitive Era

When Valorant launched in 2020, Riot Games wasted no time in pushing it toward competitive play. The first big Riot-organized event was First Strike, a series of regional tournaments held later that year. This gave fans a taste of what professional Valorant could look like while allowing Riot to test formats and establish competitive integrity. Alongside First Strike, many third-party organizers ran their own competitions, which helped develop talent and gave professional teams early experience in the new title.

    Valorant Champions Tour (VCT)

In late 2020, Riot announced the Valorant Champions Tour (VCT), a global competitive circuit that officially launched in 2021. VCT became the backbone of Valorant esports, providing a structured and long-term pathway for teams worldwide. The format is built around three major competitive stages:

  1. Challengers – Regional events where aspiring teams fight for recognition and a chance to qualify for higher stages.
  2. Masters – International tournaments held mid-season where the best from each region meet and compete for prestige, points, and prize money.
  3. Champions – The year-ending world championship, the pinnacle of Valorant esports, where qualifying teams from around the globe battle for the title of world champion.
